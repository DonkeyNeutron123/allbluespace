SAFETY

Your safety is ensured during and after the journey. Prior to the journey, you will receive basic training and learn how the basic features of the ship work. Our Blue Crew Suit (BCS) and Blue Spacewalk Suit (BSS) are both carefully crafted to ensure the safety of both the tour guide and the tourist; they are lightweight, mobile, durable, and UV blocking; all suits include survival gear in the case of unexpected situations. Our tour guide is highly trained to control the rocket as well as the roving vehicle, and you will be trained for emergencies.

Launch Procedures

The Tourists will board the Spacecraft 5-7 hours before the takeoff. The process of boarding begins several hours before the launch when which you go through a series of checks and preparations, like putting on a Space Suit, Medical checks, and reviewing the plan and process for the tour. One all the checks and preparations is done, you are then transported to the launch site where the spacecraft is. You then board the spacecraft and put on your seatbelts. During this time the Astronaut onboard with you will do a series of checks to ensure everything is working how it is supposed to. Once this is completed countdown begins and the engine are ignited.

RECOVERY

When returning back to Earth, you can experience a lot of physical to physiological changes because of the microgravity and many other factors. You will have to undergo a process of medical evaluation and rehabilitation due to not being used gravity and such. After exiting the Spacecraft you are then brought to a Medical Center that will check your vital signs, blood pressure, and heart rate. You also go through a trial of medical tests that checks your bone density, muscle strength, and cardiovascular health, as well as your vision and balance. You will need to do rehab for 3-5 days to regain strength and balance. You will also go talk to mental health professional to make sure you are getting used to Earth psychologically.
